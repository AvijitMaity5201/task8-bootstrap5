Discover Chureito Pagoda & Mount Fuji

Tucked into the quiet hills of Fujiyoshida City, the Chureito Pagoda stands like a timeless guardian watching over the valley. Built in 1963 as part of the Arakura Sengen Shrine, this five-storied pagoda isn’t ancient—but its surroundings make it feel like you’ve stepped into an old painting of Japan.Climbing the 400+ stone steps to reach the viewpoint is almost a ritual. With every step, the city shrinks behind you, the air grows cooler, and the vast silhouette of Mount Fuji becomes sharper. At the top, the world suddenly opens up: a perfect alignment of the pagoda’s red tiers framed by a sea of cherry blossoms, all resting under Fuji’s snow-capped crown.Climbing the 400+ stone steps to reach the viewpoint is almost a ritual. Explore Japan's Osaka Castle

Rising above the modern skyline of Osaka, Osaka Castle is a masterpiece of endurance.Built in 1583 by Toyotomi Hideyoshi, one of Japan’s greatest warlords.Famous for its massive stone walls, gold-trimmed roofs, and that dramatic teal color. Surrounded by a huge moat and gardens full of cherry blossoms. Originally built in 1583 by the powerful warlord Toyotomi Hideyoshi, the castle was designed to be a declaration of strength—towering walls, imposing gates, and massive moats that once intimidated anyone who dared approach.Today, that same castle stands like a jewel—its emerald-green roofs, golden ornaments, and intricate architecture glistening under the changing sky. Surrounded by Osaka Castle Park, the area turns breathtaking during spring.Inside, the castle has transformed into a modern museum, telling tales of samurai battles, political intrigue, and Osaka’s rise as a major cultural and economic hub. From the top floor, the 360-degree observation deck reveals the entire city
